So, well, let the first one be a small fact on the theory of self-adjoint operators, which i have found while going through the lecture notes on the course i take this semester.

Let $X, Y$ be Hilbert spaces and let $T \in \mathcal{B}(X, Y)$ be a self-adjoint operator. I would like to prove that $||T|| = r(T)$

First, applying the C*-identity, we claim that $||T^{*}T||=||T||$, then by induction $||T^{2^{n}}||=||T||^{2^{n}}$. The next step is to apply the Berling formula: indeed $$r(T) = \lim_{n \to \infty}{||T^{n}||^{\frac{1}{n}}}=\lim_{n \to \infty}{||T^{2n}||^{\frac{1}{2n}}}=||T||$$

Lemma: Let $A$ be a unital algebra and $a \in A$ be an idempotenent elements, i.e. satisfiying the $a^{2}=a$. Then the $\sigma(a) = \{0, 1 \}$. Proof: Let's consider the $f(x) = x-x^{2}$ Since the polynomial calculus preserves the spectrum, i.e. $\sigma(f(a))=f(\sigma(a))$, $\sigma(f(a))=\sigma(a-a^{2})=\sigma(0)=0$, on the other hand, $f(\sigma(a))=\sigma(a)-\sigma(a)^{2}=0$, then $\sigma(a) = \{0, 1 \}$.

Theorem: (Schauder compactness theorem) Let $T \in \mathcal{B}(X, Y)$, then $T$ is a compact operator iff $T^{*}$ (Banach adjoint) is a compact operator.
